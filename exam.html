What is CUET UG?

Common University Entrance Test (CUET) UG is a national-level entrance examination conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to undergraduate programs in central universities and other participating institutions across India.

Eligibility Criteria

📚 Educational Qualification

  • ✓ Passed 12th or equivalent from recognized board
  • ✓ Minimum 50% marks in qualifying exam
  • ✓ 45% for SC/ST/PwD candidates
  • ✓ Appearing candidates also eligible

🎂 Age Limit

  • 📋 No upper age limit for most courses
  • 📋 Some universities may have specific age criteria
  • 📋 Check individual university requirements

Preparation Tips & Strategy

📚 Study Strategy

  • ✓ Start with NCERT books for domain subjects
  • ✓ Practice reading comprehension daily
  • ✓ Stay updated with current affairs
  • ✓ Solve previous year papers regularly
  • ✓ Take mock tests to improve speed

⏰ Time Management

  • ⏱️ Allocate 45 minutes per section efficiently
  • ⏱️ Attempt easier questions first
  • ⏱️ Don't spend too much time on one question
  • ⏱️ Keep 5 minutes for final review
  • ⏱️ Practice with timer during preparation

📖 Free Resources

  • 📚 NCERT textbooks (Class 11 & 12)
  • 📚 Previous year question papers
  • 📚 Sample papers from NTA
  • 📚 YouTube educational channels
  • 📚 Government websites for GK

🎯 Exam Day Tips

  • 🎯 Reach exam center 30 minutes early
  • 🎯 Carry admit card and valid ID proof
  • 🎯 Read instructions carefully
  • 🎯 Stay calm and focused
  • 🎯 Avoid negative marking by guessing

Frequently Asked Questions

There is no limit on the number of attempts for CUET UG. Candidates can appear for the exam multiple times as long as they meet the eligibility criteria.

Coaching is not mandatory for CUET UG. With proper self-study using NCERT books, previous year papers, and mock tests, students can achieve good scores. However, coaching can provide structured guidance and doubt resolution.

CUET UG follows the government reservation policy: 15% for SC, 7.5% for ST, 27% for OBC-NCL, 10% for EWS, and 5% for PwD candidates. Each participating university may have additional reservation policies.

CUET UG difficulty level is moderate and is based on NCERT curriculum. It's generally considered easier than JEE Main but requires good preparation in language, domain subjects, and general knowledge.

Yes, you can change your exam center during the correction window period. However, changes are subject to availability of seats in the desired center and may require additional fees.
